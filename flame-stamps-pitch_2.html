flame stamps

a small poetic ritual for the season, point it at your everyday, and let it bloom.

the idea

for a few weeks a year, Dubai turns the colour of fire, and most people just walk past it.

flame stamps isn't about going out to find a tree. point your phone at whatever's actually in front of you, the morning coffee, the light on the dashboard, a friend's hand on the table, and frame it through one of five brand shapes drawn straight from the campaign's own mark. the ordinary moment comes out looking like something worth keeping.

each capture becomes a stamp, dated, located, and warmed through one of four duotone filters lifted from the bloom's own palette. no filters to choose, no settings to fuss over: just point, hold, and let it develop.

stamps collect into a personal album across the season. the best ones turn into a shareable story card, carrying the flame tree mark wherever they get posted.

where this could go

one tree, grown by everyone who stopped to look

every stamp captured this season could feed a second life: a single, ever-growing flame tree assembled entirely from what people send in. each submitted petal or flower becomes one more bloom on its canopy.

displayed somewhere public, a government lobby, a museum wall, it would never look quite the same twice, gaining colour in real time as the season goes on.

a quiet moderation pass keeps only on-tree, on-brand submissions in the canopy, so the installation stays beautiful no matter how many hands touch it. and everyone who contributes keeps their own bloom too, a small digital keepsake from a tree they helped grow.

each dot is a submitted bloom, the faded ones didn't make the canopy.
